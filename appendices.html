Appendix A

Historical Notes by Region

Short, high-yield notes tied to places on the route. Not a guidebook — just enough to give the trip intellectual depth.

Vancouver & the North Shore

The city sits on the unceded traditional territories of the Musqueam, Squamish, and Tsleil-Waututh Nations. European settlement accelerated after the Canadian Pacific Railway reached the waterfront in 1887, but Indigenous communities have lived here for thousands of years. Deep Cove and the North Shore were accessible mainly by boat until the Lions Gate Bridge opened in 1938. The working waterfront — sawmills, grain terminals, container port — remains visible and tells the story of a city built on resource extraction and Pacific trade.

Connection: Takaya Tours (Day 7) offers a direct link to this living history through Tsleil-Waututh guides.

Sea-to-Sky Corridor

The highway follows an ancient travel route between coast and interior. Squamish (Sḵwx̱wú7mesh) territory stretches from the head of Howe Sound into the mountains. Logging dominated the economy for a century — Parkhurst ghost town is a relic of this era, its sawmill running from 1926 to 1956 before the town emptied out. The 2010 Winter Olympics transformed Whistler from a ski town into a year-round resort and prompted massive highway upgrades that turned a notoriously dangerous two-lane road into the scenic drive it is today.

Connection: The Squamish Lil'wat Cultural Centre (Day 15) tells the story of the two Nations whose territories meet at Whistler.

Victoria & the Cowichan Valley

Victoria was established as a Hudson's Bay Company fort in 1843 and became the capital of the Colony of Vancouver Island. Its Chinatown is the oldest in Canada. The Cowichan Valley was settled by both Indigenous Quw'utsun (Cowichan) people — one of the largest First Nations communities in BC — and European farmers. The Chemainus murals tell the town's logging and community history across dozens of outdoor paintings.

Connection: Gonzales Hill (Day 18) provides a physical vantage point from which to see how the colonial city, the strait, and the Olympic Mountains relate to each other.

Cumberland, Bevan & the Coal Coast

Cumberland was founded in 1888 by coal baron Robert Dunsmuir. At its peak, it was one of BC's largest towns, with a Chinatown of over 3,000 residents. Chinese, Japanese, and South Asian workers formed the majority of the mining labour force and lived in segregated communities. Labour unrest, including major strikes in 1912–14, shaped BC's early labour movement. The mines closed progressively through the 20th century; Bevan was abandoned when its mine shut down.

Connection: The Cumberland Museum (Day 25) tells this story with care and nuance.

Sunshine Coast

The Sunshine Coast is the traditional territory of the shíshálh (Sechelt) and Tla'amin Nations. European settlement came through logging, fishing, and marine transport — the coast is still not connected to the mainland by road and depends on ferries. Pender Harbour's name comes from Captain Daniel Pender of the Royal Navy, who surveyed these waters in the 1860s. Madeira Park was named by José Gonzalos, a settler from Portugal's Madeira Islands who arrived in the early 1900s.

Weather & Smoke Monitoring

Wildfire Smoke

BC can experience wildfire smoke in July/August. Monitor firesmoke.ca and the BC Wildfire Dashboard. Coastal areas are usually less affected than the Interior but not immune. If air quality deteriorates, shift to indoor alternatives.

Forecasts: weather.gc.ca — check 2–3 days ahead for each region.

Expect: 20–28°C coast/Vancouver, 15–25°C Whistler altitude, Tofino foggy mornings clearing. Sunset ~9:15 pm late July, ~8:30 pm mid-August.

Appendix C

Food, Groceries & Local Notes

🏠 Deep Cove (Days 5–12)

Groceries: Save-On-Foods or Superstore in North Vancouver (on your way from downtown).
Breakfast: Honey Doughnuts & Goodies — go early for shorter queues.
Easy dinner: Arms Reach Bistro — waterfront, casual, family-friendly.
Treat: Honey Doughnuts (yes, again).

🏔️ Whistler (Days 13–15)

Groceries: Fresh St. Market in the village.
Breakfast: Purebread Bakery, Function Junction — slightly off-peak for shorter queues.
Easy dinner: Shed, Hunter Gather, or Old Spaghetti Factory.
Treat: Great Glass Elevator candy shop.

🏛️ Victoria (Days 17–18)

Groceries: Thrifty Foods (multiple locations).
Breakfast: Jam Café (expect a queue) or Blue Fox Café.
Easy dinner: Red Fish Blue Fish — waterfront container, fish and chips.
Treat: Rogers' Chocolates on Government Street — since 1885.

🏖️ Parksville (Day 19)

Groceries: Quality Foods, Parksville.
Quick meal: Fish and chips at any Qualicum Beach or Parksville spot.

🌊 Tofino (Days 20–23)

Groceries: Co-op in Tofino (stock up in Port Alberni if prices matter — Tofino is expensive).
Breakfast: Rhino Coffee House or Tofitian Café.
Easy dinner: Tacofino (food truck, no booking) or Shed Tofino (burgers, first-come).
Nicer dinner: Shelter (waterfront, book ahead) or Wolf in the Fog (book well ahead).
Treat: SoBo ice cream sandwich; Tofino Brewing Company for adults.

🏔️ Comox / Courtenay (Days 24–25)

Groceries: Thrifty Foods, Courtenay.
Dinner: Atlas Café, Courtenay.
Treat: Hot Chocolates, Courtenay — handmade chocolates.

⚓ Madeira Park (Days 26–29)

Groceries: IGA Madeira Park or Oak Tree Market.
Easy dinner: Triple B's Burgers; Harbour Pizza; Grasshopper Pub (waterfront).
Treat: Baked On The Coast — pastries.

🏙️ Final Vancouver (Days 30–32)

Groceries: Any Safeway, Save-On-Foods, or Whole Foods nearby.
Breakfast: Café Medina (arrive early).
Last dinner out: Granville Island — let the girls choose from the market stalls.
Treat: Earnest Ice Cream — a Vancouver classic.

Appendix E

Practical Notes

🐻 Bears

Black bears are common on Vancouver Island and along the Sea-to-Sky corridor. Store food properly, make noise on trails. Bear-aware basics apply. They are generally not aggressive — but give them space.

☀️ Daylight & Sun

Sunset ~9:00–9:15 pm late July, ~8:30–8:45 pm mid-August. Long evenings for beach walks. Bring sunscreen — BC sun at altitude (Whistler) and on water (ferries, kayaking) is strong.

🏘️ Tofino Accommodation

Book vacation rentals early — peak summer fills up months in advance. Ucluelet tends to have more availability and lower prices. Stock up on groceries in Port Alberni if budget matters.

🛣️ Highway 4

The single chokepoint between east and west Vancouver Island. Check DriveBC for conditions and closures shortly before and on the day of travel (Days 20 and 24).
