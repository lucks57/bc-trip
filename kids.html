Kids Corner

Everything to get excited about — beaches, animals, forest walks, treats, and travel games for 33 days in British Columbia

🏖️ Best Beaches for Playing

Sandy Toes & Tide Pools

  • Rathtrevor Beach, Parksville — when the tide goes out, warm shallow pools appear on the sand for hours. You can splash, build, and explore without the cold ocean waves.
  • Chesterman Beach, Tofino — long golden sand with gentle surf. At low tide, you can walk across to a small island (Frank Island) on a sandy bridge that disappears when the tide comes back!
  • Crystal Cove & Mackenzie Beach, Tofino — rocky tide pools full of sea stars, hermit crabs, anemones, and tiny fish. Bring a magnifying glass.
  • Lakeside Park, Alta Lake, Whistler — a mountain lake with a sandy beach, swimming dock, a floating raft to climb on, and a playground right there.
  • Long Beach, Pacific Rim — one of the longest beaches on Vancouver Island. Wild, wide, and magical.
✨ Best "Wow" Moments

Things You'll Remember Forever

  • Peak 2 Peak Gondola, Whistler — you float between two mountains in a glass cabin, 436 metres above the valley floor. The views go on forever.
  • Vallea Lumina, Whistler — after dark, a forest comes alive with coloured lights, music, and stories. You follow a trail through the trees and everything glows around you. It's magic.
  • Cathedral Grove — trees that were alive before Christopher Columbus was born. Some are 800 years old. You stand at the bottom and look up, and up, and up.
  • Whale watching, Victoria — seeing an orca or a humpback whale surface right beside the boat. They're enormous and gentle and they blow water into the sky.
  • Shannon Falls, Squamish — a waterfall taller than a skyscraper (335 metres!), just a 5-minute walk from the car park.
  • Riding a BC Ferry — the ferries are huge ships. You can stand on the outside deck and watch islands slide past, look for seals, and feel the wind.
🐻 Animals You Might See

Wildlife Spotting Guide

  • Black bears — on the bear-watching boat tour in Tofino, you see them walking on the beach and turning over rocks to find crabs. Safe from the boat!
  • Orcas and humpback whales — on the whale-watching tour from Victoria. Sometimes they jump right out of the water.
  • Bald eagles — you'll see them everywhere, especially around Squamish and the Sunshine Coast. Look up — they're often sitting in the very top of tall trees.
  • Harbour seals — at Seal Bay Nature Park in Comox, on ferry crossings, and around Pender Harbour. They pop their heads up and stare at you.
  • Sea stars and anemones — in the tide pools at Crystal Cove and Whytecliff Park. Orange, purple, and bright pink — they don't look real.
  • Great blue herons — tall, still, and elegant. Maplewood Flats near Deep Cove is full of them.
  • Goats on a roof — at Coombs market on Vancouver Island. Yes, real goats. On a real roof. Eating grass.
🌊 Best for Getting Wet

Splash, Paddle, Surf

  • Kayaking at Deep Cove — paddle right from the village beach into the calm inlet, with mountains rising on both sides.
  • River of Golden Dreams, Whistler — a gentle canoe route through lily pads and wetlands from one lake to another. Quiet and dreamy.
  • Surf lesson at Cox Bay, Tofino — beginner-friendly waves, full wetsuits (the water is cold!), and patient instructors. You stand up on your first lesson.
  • Swimming holes at Lynn Canyon — natural rock pools in the middle of a forest. The water is cool and crystal clear.
  • Lake swimming in Whistler — Alta Lake, Lost Lake, Alpha Lake — warm(ish) freshwater with mountains all around.
🥾 Best Short Walks

All Under 4 km — No Complaining Allowed

  • Quarry Rock, Deep Cove — 3.8 km return. Forest trail to a viewpoint over the water. The "reward view" keeps everyone motivated.
  • Rainforest Trail, Tofino — 1 km boardwalk loop through ancient rainforest. Ferns as tall as you, moss hanging from everything. Feels like a fairy tale.
  • Train Wreck Trail, Whistler — 2 km return. Ends at colourful graffiti-covered old train boxcars in the forest. You cross a suspension bridge to get there.
  • Shannon Falls, Squamish — 5-minute walk to the base of a 335-metre waterfall. The mist hits your face.
  • Wild Pacific Trail, Ucluelet — 2.6 km lighthouse loop. Rocky coast, crashing waves, and twisted trees shaped by the wind.
  • Brandywine Falls, Whistler — short walk to a platform overlooking a 70-metre waterfall dropping into a canyon.
🍩 Best Treats

The Important Stuff

  • Honey Doughnuts, Deep Cove — the queue is part of the experience. Warm, fresh, legendary. You'll want to go back every day.
  • Purebread Bakery, Whistler — pastries, cookies, and sweet things in Function Junction. Everything looks too good to eat (but you eat it anyway).
  • Tacofino, Tofino — tacos from a famous food truck. Try the cheesy bean gringa. It's messy. It's perfect.
  • Ice cream on the seawall, Vancouver — pick any vendor and walk along the water. Classic last-day move.
  • Nanaimo bars — chocolate, custard, coconut. A Canadian dessert named after the city. Try one in Nanaimo itself.
  • Rogers' Chocolates, Victoria — fancy chocolates in a beautiful old shop since 1885. You get to choose your own.
  • SoBo ice cream sandwich, Tofino — Chef Lisa's famous cookies stuffed with ice cream. Ask the locals — everyone knows about them.
  • Great Glass Elevator candy shop, Whistler Village — a candy shop. In the mountains. Need we say more?
🏠 Rainy Days & Tired Legs

When You Need to Be Inside

  • Science World, Vancouver — interactive exhibits, experiments, things to touch and build. Great for all ages.
  • Vancouver Aquarium — otters, jellyfish gallery, sea creatures up close.
  • Royal BC Museum, Victoria — dinosaur galleries, First Nations history, a woolly mammoth.
  • Ucluelet Aquarium — small, local, catch-and-release. Touch tanks where you can hold sea creatures.
  • Tofino Botanical Gardens — partly covered, children's garden, weird and wonderful art installations.
  • Squamish Lil'wat Cultural Centre, Whistler — make things, hear stories, learn about the people who have lived here for thousands of years.
  • Cumberland Museum — stories about coal miners, old photographs, a Chinatown that once had thousands of people.
  • Kids Market, Granville Island — toys, art activities, puppets, and a ball pit.
🎲 Travel Games & Car Ideas

Keeping the Car Peaceful

The longest drive is Tofino → Comox (~4 hours with stops). Most other drives are 1–2.5 hours.

🦅 BC Wildlife Bingo

Make a bingo card before the trip with: eagle, heron, seal, black bear, deer, raven, jellyfish, sea star, sea lion, orca, crab, goat (Coombs counts!). Check each one off as you spot it across the whole trip. First to fill a row wins.

⛴️ Ferry Scavenger Hunt

On each ferry crossing, find: a seagull, an island, another ferry, a kayak, a mountain with snow, a lighthouse, someone's dog, a flag. The ferries are big enough to explore — there's an outside deck, a cafeteria, and usually a play area.

📮 Postcard Project

Buy a postcard at each new place. Write one line about what you liked best. Collect them all and bring them home as a trip diary. At the end you'll have postcards from Vancouver, Deep Cove, Whistler, Victoria, Tofino, Cumberland, Pender Harbour, and more.

🌊 Tide Detective

In Tofino, you're in charge of checking the tide table each evening. Predict what the beach will look like in the morning — will the tombolo to Frank Island be there, or will it be underwater? Will there be tide pools or just waves? Keep a score of correct predictions.

🌲 Tree Ring Counting

At Cathedral Grove, find a fallen tree and count the rings. Each ring is one year. How old is it? Was it alive when your school was built? When your grandparents were born? When the first trains ran?

🗺️ Road Sign Collector

BC has distinctive green highway signs and brown park signs. See how many different types you can photograph: HWY 99 HWY 4 ⛴️ BC FERRIES — plus any animal crossing signs (watch for bear and deer warnings!).

🤓 Fun Facts About BC

  • British Columbia is bigger than France, Germany, and the Netherlands combined.
  • The trees at Cathedral Grove started growing around the year 1200 — before Gutenberg's printing press, before the Aztec Empire, before anyone in Europe had ever seen a tomato.
  • Orcas (killer whales) aren't actually whales — they're the largest members of the dolphin family.
  • The Sea-to-Sky Highway is only 120 km long but climbs from sea level to mountain altitude. That's like driving from the beach to the top of a mountain in about an hour.
  • BC Ferries carries about 22 million passengers a year — that's more than the entire population of the Netherlands.
  • Black bears can run faster than a horse over short distances. But they're usually much more interested in berries than in people.
  • Tofino gets about 3.3 metres of rain per year. That's taller than two grown-ups standing on each other's shoulders.
  • The name "Nanaimo" comes from the Snuneymuxw First Nation word meaning "a large, strong tribe."
